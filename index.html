The links worth saving

Crisis lines, pension systems, 457 providers, and survivor benefits — official sources only, gathered in one place so nobody has to dig for them at a bad moment. Built For More isn't affiliated with or paid by any of them.

Mental Health & Crisis Lines

Every one of these is confidential and answered by people who understand the job. If it's 2 a.m. and you need a person, start here. If someone at your station is struggling, this is the list to hand them.

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline Anyone, anytime — 24/7 Call or text 988
  • IAFF Center of Excellence Residential treatment for IAFF members — PTSD, substance use, behavioral health 855-900-8437
  • Fire/EMS Helpline NVFC Share the Load — firefighters, EMS, and their families 1-888-731-FIRE (3473)
  • Copline Law enforcement — answered by retired officers, 24/7 1-800-COPLINE (267-5463)
  • Safe Call Now All public safety personnel and family members — 24/7 crisis referral 206-459-3020
  • Crisis Text Line Free, confidential, text-based support for emergency responders Text BADGE to 741741

Deferred Comp Providers

Survivor Benefits

Line-of-Duty Support

There's no enrolling in these ahead of time — your family files a claim if the worst happens. The job today is knowing they exist and writing them into your Family Pre-Plan so your people aren't researching from zero.

  • PSOB — Federal BenefitsDeath, disability, and education benefits from the DOJ
  • National Fallen Firefighters FoundationSurvivor support and state-by-state benefit guides
  • Concerns of Police SurvivorsPeer support for law enforcement families

Who's behind it

Built by someone still on the job

Built For More isn't a finance influencer or a guy who left the job to sell you a course. It's run by an active-duty firefighter and company officer — someone who sits on a pension board, negotiates collective bargaining agreements, and teaches members how their benefits actually work.

That matters because your benefits aren't generic. Pension multipliers, DROP rules, and 457 options vary by state, plan, and contract. This is education from the inside — for firefighters, law enforcement, EMS, dispatch, and corrections.
